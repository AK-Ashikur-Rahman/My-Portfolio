About

Ashiqur Rahman, also known as Abul Kasem, was born on March 27, 2002, in Brahmanbaria, widely regarded as the cultural capital of Bangladesh. He comes from a distinguished musical family—his late father, Afzalur Rahman, was the world’s first blind sarod maestro, and his mother, Anwara Rahman, is recognized as the first female sarod player in Bangladesh.

Despite being visually impaired, Ashiqur and his two sisters, both accomplished North Indian Classical musician and writers, have become pillars of strength for their family. From a young age, he demonstrated exceptional musical ability, mastering classical instruments such as the sarod, sitar, tabla and violin.

Ashiqur received the National Children’s Award in 2014 and was recognized by the Shilpakala Academy in 2018. He has performed on both national and international platforms, including BTV, MYTV, CTV, Nexus TV, and prestigious venues like the National Art Gallery of Singapore.He also performed in India, Malaysia, Indonesia in various community based programs.

In 2022, he was honored with the Oikko Channel I Music Award (One of the prestigious musical awards in the subcontinent), further cementing his reputation as a rising talent in classical music. Alongside his artistic pursuits, Ashiqur remains dedicated to his education, viewing it as an essential part of his personal and professional growth.
